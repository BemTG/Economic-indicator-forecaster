The Composite Leading Indicator(CLI)

The CLI

  • The Composite Leading Indicator (CLI) is designed to provide early signals of turning points in business cycles. It is believed by some economist that the CLI can infer the future economy in advance of the business cycle by 6-9 months.
  • The indicator is mainly used by businesses and investors to help plan their activities around the expected performance of the economy and protect themselves from economic downturns.
  • It is important to realise that CLI figures are gathered qualitatively but aggregated and presented quantitatively. New figures for the CLI are published every month

What are we actually looking at?

  • What we are looking at is an aggregate of economic information from 37 countries.
  • Some of the short term indicators aggregated include: Business Confidence surveys, Orders, Inventory numbers, share prices of individual stock market indexes and businesses etc

How do we use the CLI?

  • When the CLI is above the 100 line that means the economy is expanding whereas when it is below tje 100 the economy is contracting.
