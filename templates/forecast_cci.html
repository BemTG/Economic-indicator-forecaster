The Consumer confidence index(CCI)

The CCI

  • The consumer confidence index aims to track developments in households
  • The main focus of the CCI is consumer consumption and savings, other factors such as households spending power and financial health is also accounted for.
  • Many Economist believe that the CCI is a lagging indicator and mainly use it to confirm trends in the general economy. Since consumers accounts for a big portion of the Global GDP it is an important indicator for where the economy is at for the moment.
  • The CCI is used by many individuals including manufacturers, retailers, banks and the government to aid them when making business decisions. For example, if the index rises by 10% then manufacturers may increase production accordingly to meet the anticipated consumer demand.

What are we actually looking at?

  • What we are looking at is an aggregate of economic information from 37 countries.
  • Around 40% of the CCI comprises of consumer opinions on current economic condition and 60% on consumer expectation of future economic conditions.
  • Some of the CCI survey questions include: current business condition, business condition for next 6 months, current employment condition, employment condition for the next 6 months, total family income for next 6 months...etc.

How do we use the CLI?

  • When the CCI is above 100 it indicates a boost in consumer confidence towards the future economy, hence they are more likely to spend more and save less (consumers tend to do major purchases within 12 months).
  • When CCI is below 100 consumers are more pessimistic towards future developments, as a result consumers will save more and consume less.
